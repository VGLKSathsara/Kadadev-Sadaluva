Sacred Sites

Temples Around Us

Two of Sri Lanka's most revered pilgrimage sites, walking distance from your door

5 min walk

Kataragama Devalaya

One of Sri Lanka's most sacred multi-religious pilgrimage sites, revered by Buddhists, Hindus, Muslims and Veddas alike. The sacred precinct hosts ancient shrines, fire-walking ceremonies, and the vibrant Esala Perahera festival.

5 min walk Open all day Must visit
8 min walk

Kirivehera Temple

An ancient Buddhist dagoba (stupa) believed to have been built by King Mahasena in the 3rd century BC. Kirivehera is one of the most sacred Buddhist shrines in Sri Lanka, attracting thousands of pilgrims each year, especially during Poya days.

8 min walk Open all day Sacred stupa
Wildlife Adventure

Yala & Weheragala Safari

Daily jeep safaris to Sri Lanka's most famous wildlife reserves. We arrange jeeps & experienced guides.

Sri Lanka's No.1 Park

Yala National Park

Home to the world's highest density of leopards. Also spot elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and over 200 bird species. Morning & evening safaris available.

Hidden Gem

Weheragala National Park

A tranquil reservoir safari with stunning birdlife, wild elephants coming to drink at dusk, and breathtaking scenery. Less crowded than Yala — a true nature lover's experience.
